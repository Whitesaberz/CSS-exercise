The Writing Systems

  • Hiragana
  • Katakana
  • Kanji

Hiragana ひらがな

Hiragana is a type of alphabet the Japanese developed to be able to phonetically spell out Japanese characters "Kanji"(Looks more rounded, like cursive).

Katakana カタカナ

Katakana was created as Japan finally opened it's borders in the mid 1800's to phonetically spell out foreign words in a Japanese speech fashion. (They had to do this to translate sounds they weren't used to into something they could understand). It has a much more boxy and angular style than Hiragana.

Kanji 漢字

Kanji is where things get complex; Each character has it's own meaning and often times more than one pronunciation. When you put two or more Kanji together, they can have an entirely different meaning. For example: The word "teach" is a combination of the characters for "child", "old", and "hit".
